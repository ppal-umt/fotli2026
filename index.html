Background & Objective

The FESTIVAL OF TEACHING AND LEARNING IDEAS (FOTLI) 2026 is an innovation competition specifically designed to test the readiness, creativity, and capability of academic staff and students in exploring innovative solutions.

Organized by Centre for Fundamental and Continuing Education (PPAL), Universiti Malaysia Terengganu (UMT), in strategic collaboration with the Malaysian English Language Teaching Association (MELTA) Terengganu Chapter.

The primary focus is to empower teaching methods, strengthen the delivery system, and increase productivity in the teaching and learning (PdP) ecosystem.

To ensure inclusive and sustainable participation, considering current technological advancements, FOTLI 2026 is executed fully online.

Our Journey

2020 - 2023

Started as an annual innovation competition among Centre for Fundamental and Continuing Education (PPAL) staff only.

2024

Hari Inovasi PPAL 4.0 was expanded and participation was opened to external participants.

2025 - Present

Hari Inovasi PPAL was rebranded as the FESTIVAL OF TEACHING AND LEARNING IDEAS (FOTLI) to broaden its impact on the educational community.

Rules & Regulations

Please read carefully before submitting your entry to avoid disqualification.

Participation Format

  • Individual or in groups (Maximum of 5 members).
  • Combination of lecturers/teachers and students is NOT ALLOWED.

Video Submission

  • Duration: 5-7 minutes.
  • Language: Malay or English only.
  • Format: Must be submitted via a YouTube link.
  • Encouraged to use the provided video front display template.

Abstract / Description

  • Must cover: Issues, Objectives, Implementation, and Impact.
  • Maximum length: 250 words.

Strict Data Policy

  • Information submitted CANNOT BE CHANGED.
  • Certificates printed based on provided info. Amendments/reprints incur charges. No refunds for mistakes.

Evaluation Criteria

50

Innovation Practices & Usability

Creativity and originality, practicality, relevance, participant's involvement, and potential for development.

30

Impact

Tangible positive impacts on students, specific programs, or the organization as a whole.

20

Video Presentation

Clear, creative, highly engaging delivery, and overall production quality.
